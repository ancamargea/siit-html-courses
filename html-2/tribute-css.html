Emil Racoviță

The first Romanian to have gone on a scientific research expedition to the Antarctic

A time line of Emil Racoviță's life:

  • 1868 - Born in Iași, United Principalities of Moldavia and Wallachia.

  • 1889 - Obtains a law degree from the Univeristy of Paris. He did not pursue a law career, instead turning to the natural sciences.

  • 1891-1896 - Earns a B.S. degree in 1891, and a Ph.D. degree in 1896, for a thesis on Le lobe cephalique et l’encéphale des Annélides Polychète ("The cephalous lobe and the encephalon of polychaetous annelids").

  • 1897 - Racoviță is selected to be part of an international team that started out on a research expedition to Antarctica, aboard the Belgica.

  • 1897-1899 - Emil Racoviță collects scientific data in order to conduct investigations and experiments. He was the first researcher to collect botanical and zoological samples from areas beyond the Antarctic Circle. He found the first flowering plants that were collected in Antarctica, and collected the type specimens of the flightless midge Belgica antarctica, the only insect that can survive year-round in Antarctica.

  • 1899 - Belgica was caught between ice blocks, making it impossible to sail any further. The crew had to carve a 75-meter-long canal through a 6-meter-thick layer of ice, in order to generate a waterway by which to sail to a navigable body of water.

  • 1900 - The results of Racoviță's research are published under the title La vie des animaux et des plantes dans l'Antarctique ("The life of animals and plants in Antarctica").

  • 1904 - Discovers new species of crustaceans in the Cueva del Drach cave in Mallorca. He is fascinated by this new field of science and gives up oceanology so he can dedicate his time to studying underground ecosystems.

  • 1907 - He publishes his first important research dedicated to biospeology in the world.

  • 1919 - Becomes head of the biology department at the Upper Dacia University (now Babeș-Bolyai University) in Cluj-Napoca.

  • 1920 - Racoviță establishes in Cluj-Napoca the first Institute of Speleology in the world with the help of his colleague and friend, René Jeannel.

  • 1945 - After World War II, Racoviță made great efforts to reorganize the Institute of Speology.

  • 1947 - Dies in Novemaber in Cluj-Napoca, and is buried in the city's Hajongard Cemetery.


“On the endless stairway of the times, slowly mankind is labouring, when drawn down into the darkness by the superstitions of ignorance, when drawn up to light by the truths of science… To know or not to know, that is the question! To know is to humanity: solid organization, rational activity, cooperation, solidarity, peaceful evolution. To know, for human is to spend the time of being with happiness and waiting for the moment of being serene.”

-- Emil Racoviță
